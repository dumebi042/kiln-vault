Introduction
Introduction
Sigma Prime was commercially engaged to perform a time-boxed security review of the Kiln smart contracts.
The review focused solely on the security aspects of the Solidity implementation of the contract, though general
recommendations and informational comments are also provided.
Disclaimer
Sigma Prime makes all eﬀort but holds no responsibility for the ﬁndings of this security review. Sigma Prime does
not provide any guarantees relating to the function of the smart contract. Sigma Prime makes no judgements
on, or provides any security review, regarding the underlying business model or the individuals involved in the
project.
Document Structure
The ﬁrst section provides an overview of the functionality of the Kiln smart contracts contained within the scope
of the security review. A summary followed by a detailed review of the discovered vulnerabilities is then given
which assigns each vulnerability a severity rating (see Vulnerability Severity Classiﬁcation), an open/closed/resolved
status and a recommendation. Additionally, ﬁndings which do not have direct security implications (but are po-
tentially of interest) are marked as informational.
Outputs of automated testing that were developed during this assessment are also included for reference (in the
Appendix: Test Suite).
The appendix provides additional documentation, including the severity matrix used to classify vulnerabilities
within the Kiln smart contracts.
Overview
Kiln DeFi is a protocol that allows integrators to run ERC-4626 vaults on behalf of their end users.
These vaults interact with various DeFi protocols on numerous diﬀerent chains to generate income for users.
This includes popular platforms such as Aave and Compound on Ethereum mainnet, as well as forks and other
unique projects on various Layer 2 networks.
The vaults are designed to allow integrators to have a degree of freedom over what fees to charge for manage-
ment and performance, while also being able to adhere to any legal restrictions inherent to their jurisdiction.

Scope
The review was conducted on the ﬁles hosted on the deﬁ-integrations repository.
The scope of this time-boxed review was strictly limited to ﬁles at commit 85bﬀ44. The ﬁxes of the identiﬁed
issues were assessed at commit d591799.
Note: third party libraries and dependencies, such as OpenZeppelin, were excluded from the scope of this assessment.
Approach
The manual review focused on identifying issues associated with the business logic implementation of the con-
tracts. This includes their internal interactions, intended functionality and correct implementation with respect
to the underlying functionality of the Ethereum Virtual Machine (for example, verifying correct storage/memory
layout).
Additionally, the manual review process focused on identifying vulnerabilities related to known Solidity anti-
patterns and attack vectors, such as re-entrancy, front-running, integer overﬂow/underﬂow and correct visibility
speciﬁers.
For a more detailed, but non-exhaustive list of examined vectors, see [1, 2].
To support this review, the testing team also utilised the following automated testing tools:
• Mythril: https://github.com/ConsenSys/mythril
• Slither: https://github.com/trailofbits/slither
• Surya: https://github.com/ConsenSys/surya
• Aderyn: https://github.com/Cyfrin/aderyn
Output for these automated tools is available upon request.
Coverage Limitations
Due to a time-boxed nature of this review, all documented vulnerabilities reﬂect best eﬀort within the allotted,
limited engagement time. As such, Sigma Prime recommends to further investigate areas of the code, and any
related functionality, where majority of critical and high risk vulnerabilities were identiﬁed.

KLN2-01
Deposit() and Withdraw() Vault Functions Frequently Revert
Asset
Vault.sol
Status
Resolved: See Resolution
Rating
Severity: High
Impact: Medium
Likelihood: High
Description
Calls to deposit() and withdraw() can frequently revert due to an interaction between the Vault ERC-4626 share
system and additional assets gained from standard connector activities. This can interfere with end users attempting
to withdraw or deposit to the related vault.
While users can still exit or enter positions using the share-exact mint() or redeem() functions, it is more likely end
users would like to specify their actions in the underlying asset via deposit() or withdraw() , meaning this would
delay most user interactions.
This occurs due to the _checkPartialShares() function that is called on all user interactions to prevent users receiving
small quantities of shares that may not be economic to interact with. When a connector gains additional assets, usually
through interest accrued in the underlying investment platform, it will mint new shares via _accrueRewardFee() to
represent this.
As these new shares are minted prior to calling _checkPartialShares() , it is possible for them to change the ratio of
assets to shares in the system and so lead to the quantity of assets a user wishes to deposit or withdraw to generate
a quantity of shares that includes an undesirable small remainder amount. This will result in the overall call reverting,
blocking the intended action.
Recommendations
Several strategies can be applied to mitigate this issue:
• Remove the partial shares check, although this may leave users with small quantities of shares that are not eco-
nomic to remove or interact with;
• Round down partial share remainders when they occur;
• If no business logic changes are desired, then the vault could be used so long as all UIs point only to share-exact
mint() and redeem() functions.
Resolution
The development team has ﬁxed this issue in PR#221 by rounding down partial share remainders when they occur.
To do so, the development team has implemented a new function _roundDownPartialShares() which round down the
shares as follow:

function _roundDownPartialShares(uint256 shares) internal view returns (uint256) {
uint8 _offset = _decimalsOffset();
if (_offset > 0) {
shares -= shares % 10 ** _offset;

The changes have been applied to the functions previewDeposit() , previewWithdraw() and withdraw() .

KLN2-02
EVM PUSH0 Opcode Not Supported On Certain Layer 2s
Asset
/*
Status
Resolved: See Resolution
Rating
Severity: High
Impact: Medium
Likelihood: High
Description
Some Layer 2 networks do not support certain Opcodes and are not as up-to-date as the Ethereum mainnet's version
of the EVM. In particular, several Layer 2 networks do not support the PUSH0 opcode introduced in version 0.8.20
with the Shanghai update. Solidity code compiled with a pragma equal to or higher than 0.8.20 and EVM version
Shanghai or newer will fail to deploy to unsupported networks.
This will make contracts unavailable on certain networks. In particular, the currently aﬀected networks were:
• Linea
• Flare Network
• ZkSync
This list is not exhaustive and may grow as Kiln deploys to more chains.
Recommendations
Adding EVM version London to the compiler settings or making use of Solidity version 0.8.19 will allow contracts to
be deployed on related Layer 2 networks.
For the reason of preserving testing outcomes, restricting the EVM version is more advisable, as Foundry does not
ensure the test EVM matches the onchain EVM version when performing fork tests.
Resolution
The development team has conﬁrmed that they will use the London EVM version when deploying the project to the
mentioned chains.

KLN2-03
Non-standard ERC-20 Token Vaults Cannot be Upgraded
Asset
Vault.sol
Status
Resolved: See Resolution
Rating
Severity: High
Impact: Medium
Likelihood: High
Description
A vault with an ERC-20 token which does not strictly follow the ERC-20 token interface cannot be upgraded.
This is because the token's approve() function does not adhere to the ERC-20 interface. One prominent example for
such tokens is the stablecoin USDT which is supported by the Kiln Protocol.
When trying to upgrade the vault to the new implementation using the function upgrade() , the transaction reverts
for two reasons:
1. Certain tokens do not have a
bool return value for
approve() which is expected in the interface
IERC20 .
The result is that the call IERC20(asset()).approve() in __Vault_upgrade() on line [412] will revert due to an
incorrect return value.
For example in the case of USDT, the function does not return any value, which causes an execution error as a
bool is required.
2. Certain tokens require the allowance to be set to zero before they can be set to a non-zero value. Again USDT is
an example of this, causing a revert to occur as the previous allowance is a non-zero value.
Recommendations
Use forceApprove() from the OpenZeppelin library SafeERC20 instead of the default approve() method.
Resolution
This issue has been ﬁxed in PR#219 as recommended. The function approve() is replaced by the function forceApprove()
to avoid the revert of the non-standard ERC20 tokens.

KLN2-04
Incorrect Accounting For Additional Rewards
Asset
Vault.sol
Status
Closed: See Resolution
Rating
Severity: Medium
Impact: Medium
Likelihood: Medium
Description
Some vaults have the ability to claim additional rewards. However, as these rewards can only be claimed by the holder
of the CLAIM_MANAGER_ROLE , this will lead to rewards being allocated to the wrong users. If a user withdraws from the
vault prior to a call to claimAdditionalRewards() , they will lose any additional rewards their deposit had earnt while
in the vault. Likewise, if a user front runs a call to claimAdditionalRewards() , it is possible for them to claim additional
rewards relating to a deposit period prior to their entry to the vault.
Recommendations
Additional rewards should be accounted for using a parallel accounting system that tracks user deposit and withdrawal
times, and attributes them shares accordingly. This process would be a complex endeavour; however so some alterna-
tive solutions are:
• Making use of the claim strategy that allows additional rewards to be sent to the fee recipients address which
could then implement additional claim logic based on historic onchain data.
• Adding a delayed reward streaming system to additional rewards that can allocate them over a period of time
such as a week or a month. While the attribution of this method could still be skewed, it would help prevent
frontrunning calls and increase the predictability of such rewards.
Resolution
The development team has acknowledged this issue, with the intentions to use a parallel solution for the reward dis-
tribution.

KLN2-05
Unbounded FeeRecipient Loop Lengths
Asset
FeeDispatcher.sol
Status
Closed: See Resolution
Rating
Severity: Low
Impact: Low
Likelihood: Low
Description
The length of the _feeRecipients is unbounded: this can cause various functions that iterate through all entries of
_feeRecipients to reach the block gas limit and revert in extreme cases. While this limit is very high for Ethereum
mainnet, it is possible that for some Layer 2s the block gas limit or transaction gas limit may be much lower.
Issues can arise when updating vaults, setting new _feeRecipient arrays using setFeeRecipients() , or dispatching
accrued fees via dispatchFees() .
Recommendations
It is recommended the Kiln team make vault integrators aware of this limitation and document the eﬀective max
length of
_feeRecipients on chains vaults are deployed on. Alternatively, an artiﬁcial safe cap could be added to
_feeRecipients to ensure this issue does not arise after vault setup.
Resolution
This issue has been acknowledged.

KLN2-06
Bad Debt Recovery Will Be Charged Performance Fee
Asset
Vault.sol
Status
Closed: See Resolution
Rating
Informational
Description
In the event a connector experiences a loss; which, after some time, is recovered, the vault depositors will be charged
a performance fee on this recovery amount despite it not being a performance proﬁt.
This occurs because in the rare occasion that a connector's total assets decreases, the vault will reduce the total assets
recorded. If this happens and then the lost funds are recovered through insurance or other legal means, the vault's total
assets will rise again and the next _accrueRewardFee() will allocate the _rewardFee to the fee collector.
The result is undesirable for the vault depositors as these amounts may be either the original deposit assets or rewards
that have already been charged a reward fee.
It is noted that this scenario is likely to be rare as not all connectors are capable of realising a loss in this manner and
even those that are would need to replenish said loss due to asset recovery or insurance payout.
Recommendations
Due to the unlikelihood of this scenario occurring, the best solution may be for Kiln to monitor connectors for losses
and reach out to coordinate with the underlying protocols team to ensure any recovered losses are accounted for
correctly, or temporarily setting the rewardFee to zero in cases where losses are detected.
Resolution
This issue has been acknowledge by the developing team as it is documented and a known issue. Therefore, the testing
has reduced the severity of this issue to informational.

KLN2-07
ERC-4626 Speciﬁcation Deviation
Asset
Contract.sol
Status
Closed: See Resolution
Rating
Informational
Description
The underlying asset of an ERC-4626 vault is intended to be an ERC-20 speciﬁcation compliant token. However, several
existing vaults have underlying tokens that are not fully ERC-20 compliant, such as USDT. While a speciﬁc issue was
noted for upgrading USDT vaults, other non- ERC-20 tokens may pose diﬀerent integration risks as ERC-4626 vaults
are not intended to be used with them.
Recommendations
Vault integrators should be advised to ensure the underlying token their vault uses is ERC-20 compliant. In the event
a non- ERC-20 token is required, it should be thoroughly assessed to ensure it will integrate correctly with all vault
contracts.
Resolution
This issue has been acknowledged by the development team.

KLN2-08
Claim Manager Can Control Additional Rewards
Asset
Vault.sol
Status
Closed: See Resolution
Rating
Informational
Description
If the additional reward strategy for a vault is set to reinvest or claim , then it is possible for the claim manager to
send these additional reward tokens to any address. While the claim manager is a trusted role, it is worth highlighting
this risk in case the controlling party is compromised.
Recommendations
Ensure the claim manager role is suﬃciently protected by making use of a multisig.
Additionally, consider adding logic that provides validation to transfer recipients and swap payloads.
Resolution
The development team has acknowledged this issue.

KLN2-09
Repeated Reward Claim Logic
Asset
KineticConnector.sol
Status
Resolved: See Resolution
Rating
Informational
Description
The Kinetic connector contains logic to claim wFLR tokens that will repeat claims redundantly, leading to waste of gas.
When calling rFLR.claimRewards() , each call will iterate through all eligible claims since the speciﬁed month until the
current month. This makes calling for each month, the current design, unnecessary.
Furthermore, every time claim() is called for wFLR rewards we always claim since the start month of the distribution
even if there has been a call before.
Recommendations
Allow the calls to claim() to store the month to process claims from in the payload input or add additional logic to
record the current month and previously claimed months in the KineticConnector contract.
Resolution
This issue has been ﬁxed in PR#220 by removing the loop that iterates through all months since deployment.

Asset

Status

Rating
Informational
Description
This section details miscellaneous ﬁndings discovered by the testing team that do not have direct security implications:
1. Single Swap Target May Be Insuﬃcient
Related Asset(s): connectors/*
Each connector makes use of an immutable swapTarget address that is the destination for swaps designed to
reinvest additional rewards earnt by the vault. Only one such address is allowed per connector and this may be
insuﬃcient if multiple reward tokens are claimed from a connector or if swap liquidity moves from one dApp to
another.
Plan ahead for connectors where multiple additional reward tokens are possible by making use of swap aggre-
gation services such as DEX aggregators, or use a self-deployed endpoint proxy so that the swap route can be
altered in the future.
2. Inaccurate Comments and Typos
Several inaccuracies and typos were noted in reviewing the codebase:
• On line [221] of Vault.sol , "addtionnal" should read "additional"
• On line [231] of Vault.sol , the comment deviates from the protocol speciﬁcation as it suggests you can
skip sanctions being used by not setting a sanctionList address. The speciﬁcations suggest the Chainalysis
sanction list is always in eﬀect. The correct approach should be reﬂected in both areas to prevent confusion.
• On line [195] of FeeDispatcher.sol , there is a misleading comment, fee splits will sum in underlyingDecimal
scaling which is not always 18 digits.
• On line [193] of AaveV3Connector.sol , the comment incorrectly mentions COMP when the connector can
have multiple diﬀerent reward assets.
• On line [24] of SUSDSConnector.sol , the comment incorrectly mentions sDAI which was the legacy token
for sUSDS.
• On line [38] of AaveV3Connector.sol , the interface should be named IPoolDataProvider not PoolDataProvider ,
to match interface naming standards.
Typos and comment inaccuracies should be ﬁxed to improve readability.
3. Magic Numbers
Related Asset(s): VaultFactory.sol
Several values are hardcoded throughout the body of the code which can make it diﬃcult to update or recognise
their purpose.
Adding hardcoded values as named constants declared at the beginning of contracts improves readability and
maintainability.

4. Unclear Pause Timestamps
Related Asset(s): ConnectorRegistry.sol
The pauseTimestamp and Paused events make use of the type max value such as type(uint256).max to denote
a paused connector. This timestamp is supposed to be the time when a connector is unpaused but with such
large values these times will never occur.
It is suggested to make their meaning clearer by replacing them with a boolean paused status.
Recommendations
Ensure that the comments are understood and acknowledged, and consider implementing the suggestions above.
